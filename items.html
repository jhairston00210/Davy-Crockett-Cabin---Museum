The First level of the Cabin

This particular Davy Crockett Cabin, made using the original wood, is decorated with scenic attire, and life-like props that have been used during Davy Crockett's life. The interior is adorned by: bear skins, well-worn beds, taxadermied animal skins, and old firearms mounted on the walls and in display cases.

The Second level of the Cabin

On the second floor of Davy's Crockett, there is a large weaving station that Davy Crockett's wife weaved clothing, balankets, as well as small pounches and other small items. On the other side of the weaving station there is a bed where Davy Crockett slept while he was alive.

The Perimeter of the Cabin

Once you apporach the cabin you are greeted by a tiny, open area of land that is filled with grass and picnic tables and surrounded by fencing. Off to the left side of the cabin there is a tombstone, representing the memory of Crockett's mother. To the right is a small bridge that crosses over a large gap into a large open area with more picnic tables.
